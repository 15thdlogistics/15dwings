1.0 Preamble & Operating Capacity

This document constitutes a binding, binary operational agreement governing all users, charterers, personal assistants, brokers, and registered air carriers (the "Operators") interacting with the 15D Wings dispatch oversight engine. 15D Wings is a proprietary mission control system and trading style owned and operated by Sky Party LTD (RC 8997727), registered under the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria.

Important Clarification: 15D Wings is not a direct air carrier. It does not own, maintain, or operate aircraft. The platform acts as an architectural safety network and mission-management coordinator that validates, monitors, and optimizes private and commercial flight dispatch commands. All flights are physically performed by civil aviation-authorized direct air carriers holding valid Air Operator Certificates (AOC).

2.0 The Five-Pillar Integrity Model

To maintain absolute flight integrity, our platform measures operational viability through five independent metrics. Failure in any single pillar triggers automated system alerts and potential dispatch holds.

01. Permits

Legal civil aviation clearances (overflights, slots, and landing permissions).

02. Aircraft

Physical positioning, mechanical flight release status, and technical log integrity.

03. Crew

Certified crew physical duty limitations, licenses, and verified on-site presence.

04. Fuel

Documentary verification of fuel commitment, Supplier Invoices, or Fuel Release Numbers (FRN).

05. Payment

Fully cleared, verified financial settlement residing within designated escrow/accounts.

3.0 Client & Personal Assistant Responsibilities

3.1 Strict Manifest Integrity & Document Validity

Clients and their authorized agents are legally bound to submit comprehensive, accurate manifest details for all passengers. Under NCAA and FAAN security protocols, names must match passport/ID data exactly as displayed on the Machine Readable Zone (MRZ). Any mismatches will result in boarding denial.

* The "6-Month Rule" is Absolute: Passport must be valid for 6 months beyond the arrival date. No exceptions are logged.

3.2 Passenger Roster Adjustments (No Last-Minute Swaps)

Unlike commercial ticket adjustments, private aviation manifests require formal airspace agency registration (NAMA in Nigeria). Any passenger roster change requested within 24 hours of scheduled departure requires full refiling, exposing the mission to immediate 45- to 90-minute slot delays. The system reserves the right to deny last-minute additions to prevent cascading route delays.

3.3 Financial Settlement Timelines

Mission payment must be fully cleared and visible in our account by T-48 Hours before departure. "Proof of transfer" slips, pending bank drafts, or conversion notices are not accepted as cleared funds. Failure to settle balance gates at T-48 initiates an automated system-wide abort sequence or immediate fallback-allocation procedures.

3.4 Cancellation Policies & Flight Credits

Voluntary client-side cancellations result in standard asset fees being processed as Flight Credits inside our operator network. Planning and advisory administrative fees accrued during the mission initiation stage (typically 10-15% of flight logistics value) are deemed **fully earned** at T-48 and are non-refundable.

4.0 Operator Code & Activation Tiers

Operators inside the 15D Wings ecosystem are categorized into three structured activation levels. Higher tiers carry binding performance duties:

TIER 1: SOFT HOLD No technical routing committed. Early stage mission planning and positioning mapping. No financial obligation.
TIER 2: WARM ACTIVATION Crew alerted, tail position noted. Permit submission queue initialized. Basic operator coordination overhead reimbursed if halted.
TIER 3: FULL ACTIVATION Aircraft locked, crew duty hours ticking, physical landing permits assigned. Real funds fully committed under non-retractable guarantees.

4.1 Mandatory Written-Only Communication

Verbal authorizations, phone declarations, or voice notes regarding fleet status, crew readiness, or maintenance compliance are contractually invalid. To maintain traceability, operators must issue explicit written status reports. Voice logs must be backed up by email or systemic textual declarations within 10 minutes.

4.2 Automated Pivot Triggers (Ecosystem Circuit Breakers)

The platform enforces automated operational "circuit breakers." A system-initiated **Pivot to Secondary Asset** occurs immediately, without exception, under the following circumstances:

  • Permit Delay Trigger: Landing or overflight permits unreleased at T-72 for international missions.
  • Slot Trigger: NAMA slot domestic confirmation is unconfirmed or remains strictly verbal at T-48.
  • Crew Legality Lock (T-24): Crew return legs reliant on unauthorized duty extensions, manual crew discretion sheets, or unpositioned relief crew.
  • Crew Physical Presence (T-12): Pilot-in-Command (PIC) or First Officer (FO) are not in the departure city at T-12.
  • PIC Responsiveness: Flight crew / PIC fails to reply to direct platform operations query with binary "YES" or "NO" within 15 minutes.
  • Divergent Reporting: Technical operators and ground crews issue conflicting diagnostic or positional reports that remain unresolved for 10 minutes.
  • Fuel Release Gate: Failure to provide a verified Fuel Release Number (FRN) or valid receipt confirmations by T-6.

4.3 Strict Anti-Renegotiation Protections

Any attempt by an Operator to modify pricing models, insert fuel surcharges, adjust aircraft tail numbers without formal structural consent, or alter general terms after Tier 3 lock will result in immediate programmatic freeze of the mission, routing to the designated Shadow Asset, and systemic review of the Operator's regional certification standing.

5.0 Broker & Intermediary Protocols

Third-party retail brokers, lifestyle agencies, and family office representatives must abide by these strict compliance rails:

  • White-Label Communication: Brokers must declare passenger profiles and secure digital forms immediately. Hiding proxy identities from regional security lists (NAMA/FAAN/NCAA) is illegal.
  • Client-Side Escrow Integrity: Brokers must mirror our strict payment visibility gates. If the client funds do not clear the broker's platform in time to settle our T-48 threshold, the system triggers the automatic pivot protocol.
  • Accurate Agency Data Inputs: Brokers hold primary legal liability for any miskeyed alphanumeric passport details, expired visa details, or missing customs addresses (P.O. Boxes are strictly invalid for APIS systems).

6.0 Fuel Assurance & Operational Verification

Jet A-1 fuel availability in Nigeria and specific international destination ramps is designated a core system risk. Our Ground Intelligence network evaluates fuel readiness in structured stages:

T-12 Hours: Operational status is set to PENDING upon upload of valid supplier proforma/invoice.

T-6 Hours: Status rises to CONFIRMED when a unique Fuel Release Number (FRN) from accredited vendors (e.g., TotalEnergies, Ardova, Oando, Shell Aviation) is entered.

T-1 Hour: Status rises to VERIFIED upon digital confirmation of fuel truck dispatch.

7.0 Limits of Liability & Absolute System Rule

THE SYSTEM RULE: "If any critical verification, dispute, or explanation requires more than 2 minutes to resolve physically or verbally, the mission command architecture automatically triggers a secondary pivot. Redundancy bypasses debate."

Privacy Policy & Secure Data Manifesto

1.0 Data Protection Framework & Jurisdiction

Because premium aviation manifests require highly sensitive Passenger Identifiable Information (PII), we maintain strict digital compliance frameworks aligned with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in Europe, and national security directives governing global Advance Passenger Information Systems (APIS).

2.0 Types of Data Captured & Use Contexts

To fulfill border control, FAA flight plans, and executive manifest requests, our systems securely capture and route the following elements:

  • Primary Identification Data: Full legal surname, given names, date of birth, gender, and nationality.
  • APIS Documents: High-resolution physical passport scans, passport number, expiration dates, visas, physical destination address, and legal country of permanent residence.
  • VVIP Health and Safety Declarations: Yellow Fever Vaccination statuses, active bio-security declarations, and direct dietary allergen metrics.
  • Luggage and Cargo Metrics: Physical weight, scale dimensions, and operational characteristics of accompanying pets or cargo packages.

3.0 Strict Digital Security Mandates

ANTI-PLAINTEXT TRANSMISSION BAN: Passenger passport details, personal addresses, or payment parameters must *never* be sent via plain text SMS, normal iMessage, or unencrypted email chains.

Our platform utilizes three secure, encrypted methodologies to gather passenger files safely:

01. Secure Client Portal

Encrypted transmission pipeline (SSL/TLS 1.3) utilizing optical-character reading engines to directly process passport scans into flight databases without raw persistent storage.

02. Locked Spreadsheets

Password-protected templates requiring PAs or family offices to submit encrypted Excel files, with access passwords issued via independent communication lines.

03. Physical "Conci-Scan"

In-person optical MRZ scanning via enterprise biometric tablets. Data is immediately populated into our CRM; no physical paper copies are kept.

4.0 Third-Party Sharing Rules & Customs Clearances

Data is shared strictly on a **need-to-know, operational basis** with:

  • Civil Aviation Regulators: NAMA, NCAA, FAAN (Nigeria), and their international counterparts (e.g., FAA, US CBP, Transport Canada) for regulatory manifest checks.
  • Direct Aircraft Crew: The Captain/PIC receives the mandatory physical manifest grid as part of the weight and balance package.
  • FBO & VIP Protocol Teams: Ground handling agents receive passenger names and transport profiles to secure tarmac clearances.

5.0 Storage Retention & Deletion Rights

All manifest data uploaded is automatically deleted or anonymized inside our core mission platform within 30 days of post-flight logs, save for mandatory tax records or FAA structural archives. Passenger representatives may request complete database expungement at any point by reaching out to our Data Protection Officer at concierge@15dwings.com.ng.
